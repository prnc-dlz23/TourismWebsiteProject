Taal Volcano

Taal Volcano is renowned for its unique, picturesque setting as a "volcano within a lake on an island within a lake," offering stunning views from the Tagaytay ridge. This active complex volcano is a popular tourist destination, but it is vital to remember the entire Volcano Island is a Permanent Danger Zone. Visitors must check the safety guidelines from the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) as access to the island remains strictly prohibited.

-Batangas

Puerto Galera

Puerto Galera, located on Mindoro Island and a designated UNESCO "Man and Biosphere Reserve," is a premier beach and diving destination celebrated for its natural harbor, pristine white-sand beaches, and rich marine biodiversity. This vibrant spot offers a range of aquatic activities, from world-class scuba diving in coral gardens to relaxing on popular shores like White Beach and lively Sabang Beach.

-Oriental Mindoro

Pagsanjan Falls

Pagsanjan Falls is a popular destination located in Laguna province. It is known for its beautiful three-tiered waterfall and the gorge that surrounds it. Visitors can take a boat tour to see the falls and the scenic environment.

-Laguna
