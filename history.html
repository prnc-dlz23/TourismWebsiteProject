History & Culture

Discover the diverse adventures waiting for you in Calabarzon

CALABARZON, an acronym for its five component provinces: Cavite, Laguna, Batangas, Rizal, and Quezon, is one of the Philippines' most historically significant regions. The area played a crucial role during the Spanish colonial period, with Cavite serving as the cradle of the Philippine Revolution. It was here where Filipino revolutionaries fought for independence, led by national heroes like Emilio Aguinaldo.

The region's name was officially adopted in 2002 when Southern Tagalog was divided into CALABARZON and MIMAROPA. Throughout American and Japanese occupations, Calabarzon continued to be a center of resistance and cultural preservation. The region has witnessed numerous historical events that shaped the nation's destiny, from the first proclamation of Philippine independence to battles that defined Filipino resilience.

Cultural Highlights

Festivals & Celebrations

  • Pahiyas Festival - Lucban, Quezon's colorful harvest festival
  • Higantes Festival - Angono, Rizal's giant puppet parade
  • Coconut Festival - San Pablo, Laguna's tribute to coconuts
  • Regada Festival - Cavite City's water festival
  • Anilag Festival - Laguna's celebration of its namesake lake
  • Batangas Foundation Day - Celebrating Batangas' rich heritage

Traditional Crafts

  • Buri Weaving - Quezon's intricate palm leaf crafts
  • Pottery - Traditional clay works from Rizal
  • Bamboo Crafts - Laguna's sustainable bamboo products
  • Embroidery - Lumban, Laguna's delicate embroidery works
  • Wood Carving - Paete, Laguna's religious sculptures
  • Hat Making - Cavite's traditional headwear production

Traditional Foods

  • Buko Pie - Laguna's famous young coconut pie
  • Lomi - Batangas' thick noodle soup
  • Kapeng Barako - Batangas' strong liberica coffee
  • Kesong Puti - Cavite's soft white cheese
  • Pancit Habhab - Quezon's street-style noodles
  • Minukan - Rizal's traditional rice cakes
